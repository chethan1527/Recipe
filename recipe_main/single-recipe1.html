Noodles

Noodles, an ancient and beloved staple of global cuisine, offer a tantalizing marriage of simplicity and versatility. Whether slender strands of Italian pasta, comforting bowls of Japanese ramen, or the wide variety of Asian, Middle Eastern, and Western noodle dishes in between, these doughy creations have woven themselves into the culinary fabric of countless cultures. Their ability to absorb flavors and textures, from savory broths to rich sauces, makes noodles a canvas for culinary creativity. From quick and comforting weeknight dinners to elaborate and celebratory feasts, noodles have transcended borders, becoming a symbol of comfort, convenience, and culinary artistry that continues to delight taste buds worldwide.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : breakfast food

instructions

step 1

Start by combining flour and water in a mixing bowl. The exact ratio may vary depending on the type of noodles you want to make, but a common ratio is one cup of flour to about 1/3 to 1/2 cup of water. Knead the mixture until it forms a smooth, elastic dough. This may take some time and effort to achieve the right consistency. If the dough is too dry, add a little more water; if it's too sticky, add a bit more flour. Once the dough is ready, cover it and let it rest for about 30 minutes to allow the gluten to relax.

step 2

After the resting period, divide the dough into smaller portions to make it more manageable. Roll out each portion into a thin, flat sheet using a rolling pin or pasta machine. You can dust the dough with a bit of flour to prevent sticking. Depending on your preference, you can cut the dough into various shapes and sizesâ€”thin ribbons for linguine, wide sheets for lasagna, or even shape them into dumpling wrappers or noodles with a knife or pasta cutter.

step 3

Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil and add a pinch of salt. Carefully add your freshly made noodles to the boiling water. Fresh noodles usually cook much faster than dried ones, often in just 1-3 minutes, so keep a close eye on them. Taste for doneness; they should be tender but still have a slight bite (known as "al dente"). Once cooked to your liking, drain the noodles and rinse them with cold water to stop the cooking process. Your homemade noodles are now ready to be used in your favorite recipes or enjoyed with your preferred sauce or seasoning.

ingredients

Flour 3-4 cups

Water 2-3 litres

Salt and pepper

tools

Mixing bowl

Rolling pin or pasta machine

Knife or Pasta Cutter
