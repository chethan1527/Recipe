Shawarma

Shawarma, a tantalizing culinary delight, originates from the Middle East and has gained worldwide popularity for its irresistible combination of flavors and textures. Typically made from marinated and thinly sliced meat, such as beef, chicken, or lamb, shawarma is cooked on a vertical rotisserie, resulting in succulent, tender meat that's packed with savory spices. Served in a variety of ways, from wrapped in warm pita bread with fresh vegetables and tahini sauce to plated with rice and grilled vegetables, shawarma offers a mouthwatering fusion of savory, smoky, and aromatic tastes that make it a beloved street food and restaurant favorite across the globe.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

1hr min.

serving

5 servings

Tags : breakfast rolls food

instructions

step 1

To create delicious shawarma, start by marinating your choice of meat, such as chicken, beef, or lamb. In a large bowl, combine yogurt, olive oil, minced garlic, and a blend of spices like cumin, paprika, turmeric, and coriander. Add the thinly sliced meat to the marinade, ensuring it's well coated, and let it sit in the refrigerator for at least a few hours or overnight for the flavors to infuse.

step 2

The key to authentic shawarma is cooking the marinated meat on a vertical rotisserie. If you don't have one, you can use a grill or oven. Thread the marinated meat slices onto a skewer or place them on a grill rack, allowing any excess marinade to drip off. Cook the meat at a moderate temperature, turning it occasionally, until it's charred on the outside and tender on the inside. This process may take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the thickness of the meat.

step 3

Once the meat is cooked to perfection, remove it from the skewer or grill and let it rest briefly. To serve, thinly slice the cooked meat, creating tender, flavorful shavings. Traditionally, shawarma is assembled by placing the sliced meat inside warm pita bread or flatbreads, along with fresh vegetables like tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, and lettuce. Drizzle with tahini or garlic sauce, and if desired, sprinkle with additional spices or herbs. Roll up the shawarma wrap, securing it with foil or parchment paper, and enjoy the delectable combination of flavors and textures.

ingredients

1.5 pounds (450-680 grams) of thinly sliced chicken, beef, or lamb.

A mixture of yogurt, olive oil, minced garlic, and various spices like cumin, paprika, turmeric, and coriander.

Pita bread for wrapping and a selection of fresh vegetables such as tomatoes, cucumbers, and onions for garnish.

tools

Vertical Rotisserie

Sharp Knife or Meat Slicer

Skewers or Grill Rack
