Omlet

An omelet, a beloved breakfast classic, is a culinary masterpiece created by whisking eggs and cooking them in a pan until they're just set but still slightly runny on the inside. This versatile dish can be customized with an array of ingredients, from cheese, vegetables, and herbs to meats like bacon or ham, allowing for endless variations to suit individual tastes. The result is a fluffy and flavorful concoction that showcases the harmony of ingredients and the skill of the cook, making it a go-to choice for a hearty and satisfying morning meal around the world. Whether folded, rolled, or served flat, the omelet remains a timeless favorite for breakfast enthusiasts and a testament to the artistry of simple yet delicious cuisine.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : breakfast food

instructions

step 1

Begin by gathering your ingredients and tools. You'll need eggs, a non-stick skillet, butter or oil for cooking, and your choice of fillings (such as cheese, vegetables, and meats). Crack the desired number of eggs into a bowl, usually 2-3 eggs per omelet, and whisk them until the yolks and whites are well combined. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.

step 2

Heat your skillet over medium-low to medium heat and add a small amount of butter or oil to prevent sticking. Once the fat is hot and shimmering, pour the beaten eggs into the pan. Let them cook undisturbed for a minute or two until the edges begin to set.

step 3

Now, add your choice of fillings evenly over one half of the omelet. Gently lift the other half of the omelet with a spatula and fold it over the fillings to create a half-moon shape. Cook for another minute or so until the omelet is set but still slightly runny on the inside (if you prefer it more well-done, cook a bit longer). Carefully slide the omelet onto a plate, and it's ready to serve.

ingredients

Eggs (2-3, depending on preference)

Butter or oil (for cooking)

Salt and pepper (to taste)

tools

Non-stick skillet

Whisk or fork (for beating the eggs)

Spatula (for flipping and folding the omelet)
